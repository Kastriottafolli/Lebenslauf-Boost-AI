Guidance: turn weak duty statements into professional, impact-driven bullets. Follow the pattern — do not copy example metrics unless they exist in the candidate’s CV.

BEFORE → AFTER

1. Weak: “Responsible for sales”
   Strong: “Grew regional B2B sales 27% YoY by restructuring the outbound pipeline and coaching 4 reps.”

2. Weak: “Helped with the website”
   Strong: “Shipped a React redesign that cut page load time 40% and lifted sign-up conversion 12%.”

3. Weak: “Did customer support”
   Strong: “Resolved 60+ tickets/week at a 4.9/5 CSAT and built a help-center FAQ that reduced repeat tickets 18%.”

4. Weak: “Managed projects”
   Strong: “Led a cross-functional team of 6 to deliver a CRM migration on schedule; standardized after-sales workflows and shortened handoff times.”

5. Weak: “Used Excel”
   Strong: “Automated monthly forecasting reports with Excel/Power Query, cutting report production from 2 days to under 3 hours.”

6. Weak (no metric in source): “Responsible for onboarding new hires”
   Strong (qualitative, no fake metric): “Designed and ran new-hire onboarding including training plans and a buddy system; shortened time-to-productivity and aligned quality standards across teams.”

Writing rule: lead with an action verb · state the concrete action · close with a tangible outcome. Never invent metrics.
